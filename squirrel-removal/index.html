Understanding Your Squirrel Problem: Signs You Need Professional Squirrel Removal in Dublin, OH

Sometimes, the signs of a squirrel infestation can be subtle at first, but they often become more obvious over time. Recognizing these signs early can prevent more extensive damage and make the removal process smoother. If you're noticing any of the following indicators, it's likely time to contact Dublin Pest Pros for expert squirrel removal services.

Scratching or Gnawing Noises

One of the most common signs of squirrels in your attic, walls, or crawl spaces is unusual noises. You might hear scratching, scurrying, or gnawing sounds, especially in the morning or evening when squirrels are most active. Squirrels are constantly chewing to maintain their teeth, and they will gnaw on wood, insulation, and even electrical wiring. These noises can be unsettling and a clear indicator that unwanted guests have taken up residence in your home. If these noises are keeping you up at night or causing you concern, it's a strong signal to seek professional help for squirrel removal. Similar noises might also indicate other rodent issues, so a professional inspection is key to proper identification.

Visible Damage to Your Property

Squirrels can cause noticeable damage to the exterior and interior of your home. Look for chewed wood on eaves, trim, or siding. They may also damage vent covers or screens to gain access. Inside, you might find shredded insulation in your attic or damaged wiring. This damage can compromise the structural integrity of your home and create safety hazards, including fire risks if electrical wires are involved. Identifying and repairing this damage is a critical part of the squirrel removal process. The extent of the damage can vary, but even seemingly minor damage can be an indication of a larger underlying problem.

Nests or Droppings

Finding squirrel nests or droppings in your attic, chimney, or other areas is a clear sign of an infestation. Squirrels build nests using materials like leaves, twigs, insulation, and shredded fabric. Their droppings resemble small pellets and can pose health risks due to potential bacteria and parasites. If you discover either of these in unused areas of your home, it's a strong indication that you have a squirrel problem that requires professional attention. Cleaning up these materials should be done with caution due to potential health risks. It's often best to leave this to the professionals who have the proper equipment and knowledge for safe disposal.

Sightings of Squirrels Entering or Exiting

If you've actually seen squirrels entering or exiting your home through a specific opening, this is a definitive sign of an infestation. Pay attention to where they are gaining access so you can share this information with our technicians. Observing their behavior can also provide clues about the severity of the problem and the location of their nest. They often use the same entry and exit points repeatedly, creating well-worn paths. Noting these patterns can significantly aid in the trapping and exclusion process. Don't try to block the entry point yourself without expert help, as this could trap the squirrels inside.

Unpleasant Odors

Dead squirrels within your walls or attic can create strong, unpleasant odors as they decompose. Similarly, an accumulation of squirrel droppings and urine can also produce foul smells. If you notice persistent, unexplained odors in certain areas of your home, it could be a sign of a hidden squirrel problem. These odors can permeate through your home, making it uncomfortable to live in. Identifying the source of the odor is important, and often, it points directly to a pest infestation that needs to be addressed promptly.

If you've observed any of these signs, don't delay in seeking professional help. Addressing a squirrel problem early can prevent further damage and make the removal process more straightforward. Beyond Removal: Preventing Future Squirrel Problems

Once we've successfully removed the squirrels from your property, our goal is to help you take steps to prevent them from returning. Prevention is key to maintaining a squirrel-free environment. Here are some important measures to consider:

Sealing Entry Points

As mentioned earlier, sealing off entry points is crucial for prevention. This includes repairing damaged areas, reinforcing vent covers and chimney caps, and ensuring that all potential access points are secured. We can help you identify and address these vulnerabilities during our exclusion service. Paying close attention to areas where different building materials meet, like where the roofline meets the siding, is important, as these areas are often susceptible to squirrel entry. Residential properties are particularly prone to these issues, but commercial buildings can also be affected.

Trimming Trees and Branches

Squirrels are excellent climbers and often use overhanging tree branches to access your roof and upper levels of your home. Trimming branches that are close to your house can significantly reduce their ability to gain entry. Maintaining a reasonable distance between trees and your structure creates a natural barrier. Consider this as part of your regular property maintenance routine. Keeping trees healthy and free of dead branches is also a good practice for overall property safety and appearance.

Securing Food Sources

Eliminating easily accessible food sources around your property can make it less attractive to squirrels. This includes securing garbage cans with tight-fitting lids, avoiding leaving pet food outdoors, and cleaning up fallen nuts and fruits from trees. If you have bird feeders, consider relocating them away from your house or using squirrel-resistant feeders. Even small food crumbs can attract these persistent critters. Being diligent about waste management is a simple yet effective preventive measure.

Considering Fencing and Barriers

Depending on the layout of your property and the level of squirrel activity, you might consider installing fencing or barriers around gardens or other areas you want to protect. While fencing may not be a foolproof solution for preventing squirrels from accessing your entire property, it can be effective in deterring them from specific areas where they are causing damage, such as vegetable gardens or flower beds. There are various types of fencing materials and designs that can be more effective against squirrels.

Professional Perimeter Treatments (Optional)

In some cases, we may recommend professional perimeter treatments designed to deter squirrels from approaching your home. These treatments can create a less inviting environment for squirrels around the exterior of your property. This is often used in conjunction with other exclusion and prevention methods for a multi-layered defense against future infestations. Discussing these options with our technicians can help you determine if they are suitable for your specific needs and property.

Taking these preventive measures after successful squirrel removal is key to maintaining a pest-free environment. Common Misconceptions About Squirrel Control

There are many myths and misconceptions surrounding squirrel control that can lead people to use ineffective or even harmful methods. Understanding the truth about squirrel behavior and effective removal is crucial.

'They'll Just Leave on Their Own'

While it's true that some animals might eventually move on, squirrels often establish territories and nests, especially if they've found a secure and comfortable place in your home. Waiting for them to leave on their own is rarely an effective strategy and often allows the problem to worsen, leading to more extensive damage and potential health risks. Once they've found a suitable nesting site, such as an attic with insulation, they are unlikely to abandon it voluntarily. Prompt action is usually the best course of action to minimize the impact of an infestation.

'DIY is Just as Effective'

While there are many DIY pest control products and methods available, tackling a squirrel infestation, especially when they are inside your home, is best left to professionals. Squirrels can be aggressive when cornered, and dealing with them requires specialized knowledge and equipment for humane removal and effective exclusion. Attempting DIY removal can also be dangerous, potentially resulting in injury to yourself or the animal. Professional pest control services have the experience to handle these situations safely and efficiently. Trying to handle it yourself could also lead to improper sealing of entry points, allowing the squirrels to return.

'All Squirrels Carry Rabies'

While it's important to exercise caution around any wild animal, rabies is relatively uncommon in squirrels. However, they can carry other diseases and parasites that can be transmitted to humans or pets through bites, scratches, or contact with their droppings. It's always safest to avoid direct contact with squirrels and their nesting materials. A professional pest control service can handle the removal and cleanup safely, minimizing any health risks. It's better to be safe than sorry when dealing with wildlife, even those not typically associated with rabies.

'Using Poison is a Quick Fix'

Using poison for squirrel control is highly discouraged and often illegal due to the risks it poses to non-target animals, including pets and wildlife, as well as potential environmental contamination. Poisoning squirrels can lead to them dying in inaccessible areas within your home, causing odor issues and making cleanup difficult. Humane trapping and exclusion methods are the preferred and more effective long-term solutions for squirrel removal. Responsible wildlife management focuses on humane and environmentally conscious approaches.

Understanding these common misconceptions can help you make informed decisions about addressing your squirrel problem and choosing the most effective and responsible approach.
